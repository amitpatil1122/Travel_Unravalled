Discover the Spiritual Side of Maharashtra:
Visit These Devotional Places


"Welcome to Maharashtra, a land of diverse cultures and rich spiritual heritage. Maharashtra is home to numerous devotional places that attract devotees from all over the world. With its unique blend of history, culture, and spirituality, Maharashtra is a must-visit destination for those seeking a spiritual experience.Experience the peacefulness and serenity of Maharashtra's spiritual retreats, like the Osho Meditation Resort, Vipassana Meditation Centre, and the Art of Living Centre. These centres offer a unique opportunity to learn and practice meditation and yoga, helping you find inner peace and tranquillity."

01. Siddhivinayak Mandir

Siddhivinayak Temple is one of the most famous and revered Hindu temples located in Prabhadevi, Mumbai. It is dedicated to Lord Ganesha, who is also known as Siddhivinayak. The temple is known for its beautiful architecture and is a popular pilgrimage site for devotees... who believe that offering prayers to Lord Ganesha at this temple can fulfill their wishes. The temple attracts millions of devotees every year, especially during the Ganesh Chaturthi festival.

02. Shirdi

Shirdi is a small town located in the Ahmednagar district of Maharashtra. It is famous for the Shirdi Sai Baba Temple, which is one of the most visited pilgrimage sites in India. The temple is dedicated to the 19th-century saint Sai Baba, who is revered by people of all religions for his teachings... of love, compassion, and universal brotherhood. The town of Shirdi has grown around the temple, and visitors can also explore various other attractions such as the Samadhi Mandir, Dwarkamai Mosque, and Chavadi. Shirdi is a must-visit destination for those seeking spiritual solace and a glimpse into the life of Sai Baba.

03. Pandharpur

Pandharpur Temple, also known as Vitthal Temple, is a famous temple located in the town of Pandharpur in Maharashtra. It is dedicated to Lord Vitthal, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu, and his consort Rukmini. The temple is considered one of the holiest places in India, and it attracts millions... of devotees every year, especially during the Ashadi Ekadashi festival.
The temple has a unique architecture, and its black stone idol of Lord Vitthal is a significant attraction. The temple also houses various other shrines and is an essential site for devotees of Lord Vishnu. Pandharpur Temple is a must-visit destination for those seeking spiritual solace and a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of Maharashtra.

04. Trimbakeshwar

Trimbakeshwar Temple is an ancient temple located in the town of Trimbak in Maharashtra. It is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, which are considered to be the most sacred abodes of Lord Shiva. The temple is situated at the foot of the Brahmagiri Hills and is surrounded by lush greenery..., making it a serene and tranquil destination.The temple has a unique architecture, and its black stone lingam, which is considered to be a representation of Lord Shiva, is a significant attraction.
The temple is also famous for its Kumbh Mela, which is held once every twelve years and attracts millions of devotees from all over the world. Trimbakeshwar Temple is a must-visit destination for those seeking spiritual solace and a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of Maharashtra.

05. Mahalaxmi Temple

Mahalaxmi Temple is a famous temple located in the city of Kolhapur in Maharashtra. It is dedicated to Goddess Mahalaxmi, the Hindu goddess of wealth, prosperity, and good fortune. The temple is considered to be one of the Shakti Peethas, which are believed to be the most sacred abodes... of the goddess.
The temple has a unique architecture, and its black stone idol of Mahalaxmi is a significant attraction. The temple also houses various other shrines, including those dedicated to Lord Vishnu and Lord Shiva. The temple attracts millions of devotees every year, especially during the Navratri festival, when it is beautifully decorated with lights and flowers. Mahalaxmi Temple is a must-visit destination for those seeking spiritual solace and a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of Maharashtra.

06. Khandoba Temple - Jejuri

Jejuri Temple is a famous temple located in the town of Jejuri in Maharashtra. It is dedicated to Lord Khandoba, who is considered to be an incarnation of Lord Shiva and is worshipped as the god of war and victory. The temple has a unique architecture, and its idol of Lord Khandoba is a significant attraction....
The temple also houses various other shrines, including those dedicated to Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati. Jejuri Temple is famous for its vibrant and colorful celebrations of the Bhandara Festival, which is held twice a year and attracts thousands of devotees from all over the world.

07. Dehu

Dehu is a small town located in the Pune district of Maharashtra. It is considered to be a significant pilgrimage site for devotees of the 13th-century saint and poet, Sant Tukaram. The town is home to the famous Tukaram Gatha Mandir, which is dedicated to Sant Tukaram and houses a collection of his poems and writings. The temple is an essential site for devotees of Sant Tukaram... and attracts thousands of visitors every year, especially during his birth anniversary, which is celebrated with great fervor and enthusiasm. Dehu is also known for its tranquil and scenic surroundings, with the Indrayani River flowing through the town, making it an ideal destination for those seeking a peaceful and spiritual retreat.

08. Alandi

Alandi is a small town located in the Pune district of Maharashtra. It is considered to be a significant pilgrimage site for devotees of the 13th-century saint and poet, Sant Dnyaneshwar. The town is home to the famous Sant Dnyaneshwar Samadhi Mandir, which is dedicated...to Sant Dnyaneshwar and houses his samadhi or final resting place. The temple is an essential site for devotees of Sant Dnyaneshwar and attracts thousands of visitors every year, especially during his birth anniversary, which is celebrated with great fervor and enthusiasm. Alandi is also known for its tranquil and scenic surroundings, with the Indrayani River flowing through the town, making it an ideal destination for those seeking a peaceful and spiritual retreat.

09. Prati Shirdi

Prati Shirdi Sai Baba Temple is a popular temple located in the town of Shirgaon in Maharashtra. It is a replica of the famous Shirdi Sai Baba Temple and is dedicated to Sai Baba, a revered saint and spiritual leader of the late 19th and early 20th centuries. The temple has a unique architecture...and is adorned with beautiful carvings and sculptures.The temple also has various other shrines, including those dedicated to Lord Ganesha,Lord Shiva, and Goddess Durga.Prati Shirdi Sai Baba Temple attracts a large number of devotees every year, especially during the auspicious occasions of Guru Purnima and Shirdi Sai Baba's birth and death anniversaries,which are celebrated with great devotion and enthusiasm.

10. Prati Balaji

Prati Balaji Temple is a famous temple located in the town of Ketkawale near Pune in Maharashtra. It is a replica of the famous Tirupati Balaji Temple located in Andhra Pradesh and is dedicated to Lord Venkateswara, a revered Hindu deity. The temple has a unique architecture...and is adorned with beautiful carvings and sculptures.The temple also has various other shrines, including those dedicated to Lord Shiva, Lord Ganesha, and Goddess Lakshmi. Prati Balaji Temple attracts a large number of devotees every year, especially during the auspicious occasions of Vaikunta Ekadashi, Brahmotsavam, and Tirumala Tirupati Brahmotsavam, which are celebrated with great devotion and enthusiasm.
