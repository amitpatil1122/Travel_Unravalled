Famous Festivals of Maharashtra You Should Witness!


"Maratha rulers left behind grandeur and verve that enriches the cultural heritage of the state. Various cultural affiliations blend together to live harmoniously in the land of prosperity. Maharashtra is divided into small regions and each region is diverse in the form of dialects, folk songs, food, ethnicity. Maharashtra is a melting pot of different races, traditions, and classes. The engaging traditions of Waghya Murali, Potraj, Vasudev, and Gondhali communities have kept alive their unique cultures and vibrant art, adding charm to the cultural vibrancy of the state. Hindus, Muslims, Jews, Buddhists, Zoroastrians (Parsis and Iranians), Christians, and Sikhs live peacefully in the state while the Deccan plateau of Maharashtra is home to tribal communities such as Bhils, Mahadeo, Kolis, Gonds, and Warlis that co-exist peacefully and follow their lifestyles and traditions. 83% of the Maharashtrian are Hindus, however, there has been a prevailing sense of secularism in the state that embraces other religions with great respect and diversity inclusion."

01. Ganesh Chaturthi

Experience the vibrant and colorful celebrations of Ganesh Chaturthi in Maharashtra, India. This ten-day festival honors Lord Ganesha, the beloved Hindu god of wisdom and prosperity. Join the festivities, witness the grand processions, and soak up the rich cultural traditions of Maharashtra... during this auspicious occasion.below are the best places to visit on "Ganesh Chaturthi".
1. Lalbaugcha Raja (Lalbaug – Central Mumbai) 2. Ganesh Galli Mumbaicha Raja (Central Mumbai) 3. Khetwadi Ganraj (Grigaim – South Mumbai) 4. GSB Seva Ganesh Mandal (King’s Circle – Central Mumbai) 5. Andhericha Raja (Andheri)

02. Gudi Padwa

Ganeshotsav is big at the Girgaon Chowpatty so it’s a no-brainer that another important festival of Maharashtra is also celebrated with a lot of pomp here. Thousands of people show up to the city beach accompanying the chariot that carries the Gudi along with the beating of the drum and ... you will also find a plethora of festive food stalls here to celebrate Gudi Padwa with the right kinds of foods.

You can also just visit the area of Girgaon as a whole if the beach and its crowd gets overwhelming for you. As the hub of a lot of Maharashtrian festivals and neighbourhoods, Girgaon promises you a fulfilling Gudi Padwa experience.

03. Makar Sankranti

Also known as Uttarayan or Pongal in other parts of the country, Makar Sankranti is the celebration of the winter solstice. It is the celebration of the advent of spring, the day when the sun leaves the southern hemisphere and begins its journey in the northern hemisphere. Following the glorious tradition...of kite-flying, there can be plenty of kites seen dotting the sky on this day. Meals of gulachi poli (jaggery flatbreads) are prepared to keep everyone warm.
Small sweet laddoos (balls) made from sesame are especially prepared for this day. People visit the homes of friends and relatives, and say “Til gul ghya ani god god bola“, meaning “Accept this til-gul and speak sweetly”.

04. Ashadhi Ekadashi

Ashadhi Ekadashi is the culmination of the great Pandharpur chi Vari (Journey to Pandharpur). Pandharpur is a town in Maharashtra on the banks of the river Chandrabhaga and is considered to be the abode of Vitthal, a local form of the mighty God. Almost a month in advance, millions... of people begin a pilgrimage to visit the holy city on foot. It is a sight to behold as millions and millions of people throng this tiny city, driven only by their faith in the Lord.
These people known as the Warkaris (people who have undertaken this journey) chant holy songs and live in extremely minimalist conditions during the entire journey, and the atmosphere is of great devotion and mutual support as everyone’s ultimate aim is only to reach the Lord and fall at his feet.

05. Shiv Jayanti

Chattrapati Shivaji Maharaj was the greatest ruler of Maharashtra. He was known for his courage and intelligence. He laid the foundation of a Hindu empire which lasted for over two centuries before the British took over. All Maharashtrians have great reverence and regard for him and his birthday... is celebrated all across the state as Shiv Jayanti.

"Chatrapati Shivaji Maharaj was a legendary warrior, visionary leader, and a symbol of courage and resilience, whose legacy continues to inspire millions across India and beyond."

06. Diwali

Diwali, also known as the "Festival of Lights," is a five-day celebration that is widely celebrated across Maharashtra. During this festival, people decorate their homes with colorful rangolis and light up their homes with diyas (lamps). Families come together to perform traditional puja rituals,... exchange sweets and gifts, and enjoy festive food.

Firecrackers are also a popular part of the celebration, with many people lighting them to add to the festive atmosphere. Diwali is a time for people to come together and celebrate the triumph of good over evil and the importance of family and community.

07. Mahashivratri

Mahashivratri is a Hindu festival celebrated across Maharashtra to honor the god Shiva. The festival usually falls in February or March and is celebrated by visiting Shiva temples, fasting, and performing prayers and rituals. Devotees offer milk, honey, and other traditional items to the Shiva lingam,... a symbol of the god, and light diyas and incense sticks as a form of worship.

The festival is an important part of Maharashtra's cultural heritage and is celebrated with great enthusiasm and devotion. It is a time for people to reflect on the teachings of Shiva, and to seek his blessings for peace and prosperity in their lives.

08. Holi

Holi is a popular spring festival celebrated in Maharashtra with great enthusiasm and joy. The festival marks the arrival of spring and is celebrated by throwing colored powder, dancing to music, and enjoying festive food. People come together to play with colors and splash water on each other, breaking barriers... of caste and creed.

The festival is also associated with the legend of Prahlad and Holika, which symbolizes the victory of good over evil. Holi is a time for people to come together, forget their differences, and celebrate the beauty of life and the changing of the seasons.

09. Dahi Handi

Dahi Handi is a popular festival celebrated in Maharashtra that marks the birth of Lord Krishna. The festival is celebrated on the day after Janmashtami and involves the formation of human pyramids to break a pot of curd hung high in the air. This ritual reenacts Lord Krishna's childhood pranks of stealing... butter and curd from his mother's pots.

Dahi Handi is an important part of Maharashtra's cultural heritage and symbolizes the spirit of unity and teamwork. It is a time for people to come together and celebrate the joy of life and the teachings of Lord Krishna.

10. Narli Purnima

Narli Purnima, also known as Coconut Day, is a unique festival celebrated in Maharashtra's coastal regions, especially in Konkan. The festival is celebrated on the full moon day of the Hindu month of Shravan and is dedicated to the sea god Varuna. On this day, people offer coconuts... to the sea as a symbol of gratitude for a good catch and seek blessings for a prosperous fishing season ahead.

The festival is also celebrated with cultural programs, dances, and feasts. Narli Purnima is an important festival for the fishing community in Maharashtra and symbolizes their close relationship with the sea.
