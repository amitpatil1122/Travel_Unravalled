"Maharashtra's Best Hill Stations
and Trekking Tours"


"Mahabaleshwar was one of the first hill stations to be developed in Maharashtra by the British in the early 19th century. The town was established as a sanatorium for British troops and officials, and soon became a popular destination for Indian royalty and wealthy businessmen. Other hill stations such as Lonavala, Khandala, and Matheran were also developed around the same time as summer retreats for the British officials. These hill stations were connected to the nearby cities by railways, which made them easily accessible to tourists. The development of trekking trails in Maharashtra also has a colonial origin. Many of the trekking routes were originally established as trade routes or military routes during the British rule. Some of the famous trekking routes like Rajmachi Trek and Sinhagad Trek were originally established as military routes to transport goods and weapons. After India gained independence, tourism in Maharashtra started to develop rapidly. The government and private organizations started to invest in the development of infrastructure, accommodations, and amenities to promote tourism in the state. Today, Maharashtra is one of the most popular tourist destinations in India, offering a wide range of hill stations and trekking trails for visitors to explore and enjoy."

01. Mahaballeshwar Hill Station

Mahabaleshwar is a popular hill station located in the Sahyadri mountain range of Maharashtra. It is situated at an altitude of 4,718 feet above sea level and is known for its scenic beauty, lush green hills, beautiful waterfalls, and serene lakes. Mahabaleshwar is also famous for its strawberry... farms and is known as the "Strawberry Capital" of India. The town is named after the Mahabaleshwar Temple, which is located on a hilltop and is one of the major attractions in the area. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is believed to be one of the oldest temples in the region.

02. Lonavala & Khandala Hill Station

Lonavala and Khandala are two popular hill stations located in the Sahyadri mountain range of Maharashtra, about 65 km from Mumbai. The towns are situated at an altitude of about 2,000 feet above sea level and are known for their scenic beauty, lush green hills..., cascading waterfalls, and serene lakes. Lonavala and Khandala are famous for their mild climate, which makes them popular tourist destinations throughout the year. The towns are known for their scenic viewpoints like Tiger Point, Rajmachi Point, and Sunset Point, which offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes. The Bhushi Dam, located between Lonavala and Khandala, is another popular attraction in the area.

03. Kalsubai Trek

Kalsubai is the highest peak in Maharashtra, with an altitude of about 5,400 feet above sea level. It is located in the Sahyadri mountain range near the town of Igatpuri, and is a popular trekking destination among adventure enthusiasts. The trek to Kalsubai begins at the base of... the mountain, and takes you through dense forests, rocky terrains, and steep slopes. The trail is well-marked and well-maintained, but it can be challenging due to its steep incline and rocky terrain.

04. Matheran Hill Station

Matheran is a small hill station located in the Raigad district of Maharashtra, about 90 km from Mumbai. It is situated at an altitude of about 2,625 feet above sea level and is known for its serene atmosphere, pleasant weather, and lush green hills. What makes Matheran unique... is that it is an eco-sensitive region and has been declared as an automobile-free zone, making it a pollution-free destination. The town is known for its scenic beauty, and has several viewpoints that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes, like the Charlotte Lake, Panorama Point, and Echo Point. Matheran is also famous for its toy train, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and takes visitors on a scenic journey through the hills.

05. Sinhagad Trek

Sinhagad Fort is a popular trekking destination located near Pune in Maharashtra. The fort is situated at an altitude of about 4,400 feet above sea level and is known for its historical significance and stunning views of the surrounding Sahyadri Mountains. The trek to Sinhagad Fort is... a relatively easy one and can be completed in about 1-2 hours, depending on your level of fitness. The trek begins at the base of the fort and takes you through lush green forests, rocky terrains, and steep slopes. Along the way, you will come across several historical landmarks like the Kalyan Darwaza, Pune Darwaza, and Tanaji Malusare's Samadhi.

07. Raigad Fort Trek

The Raigad Fort Trek is one of the most popular trekking destinations in Maharashtra, attracting adventure enthusiasts from all over the country. The trek is challenging and takes about 3-4 hours to complete, depending on your fitness level and the route you take. The trek starts from... the base village of Pachad and takes you through dense forests, rocky terrains, and steep slopes. As you climb higher, you will be rewarded with stunning views of the Sahyadri mountain range and the surrounding landscapes.

08. Saputara Hill Station

Saputara is a hill station located in the Dang district of Gujarat, but it is situated close to the Maharashtra border and is often considered a popular weekend getaway for people living in Mumbai and Pune. It is located at an altitude of about 1,000 meters above sea level and offers stunning... views of the Sahyadri mountain range. The hill station is known for its pleasant climate, serene lakes, and lush green forests. Visitors can take a walk along the Lake Garden, which is a man-made lake surrounded by beautiful gardens and scenic trails. There is also a ropeway that offers stunning views of the valley below.

09. Chikhaldara Hill Station

Chikhaldara is a hill station located in the Amravati district of Maharashtra. It is situated at an altitude of about 1,118 meters above sea level and is surrounded by dense forests, cascading waterfalls, and scenic lakes. The hill station is known for its cool and pleasant climate..., making it a popular summer retreat for tourists. Visitors can take a stroll along the Arthur Point, which offers stunning views of the surrounding valley and the Melghat Tiger Reserve.

10. Visapur Trek

Visapur Fort is a hill fort located near the hill station of Lonavala in Maharashtra. It is situated at an altitude of about 1,084 meters above sea level and is known for its historical significance and stunning views of the surrounding valley. The trek to Visapur Fort is a popular activity... among adventure enthusiasts and offers a moderate level of difficulty. The trek begins from the base village of Lohagadwadi, which is about 10 kilometers away from Lonavala. The trek takes about 2-3 hours to complete and involves climbing steep steps, traversing through dense forests, and crossing a small stream.
