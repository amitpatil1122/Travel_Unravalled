Wildlife Sanctuaries &
National Parks in Maharashtra


"Maharashtra is home to several wildlife sanctuaries, each with its unique flora and fauna. A wildlife sanctuary tour in Maharashtra typically involves visiting places like Tadoba Andhari Tiger Reserve, Pench Tiger Reserve, Nagzira Wildlife Sanctuary, Melghat Tiger Reserve, and Bhimashankar Wildlife Sanctuary. Visitors can expect to see a wide variety of animals, including tigers, leopards, sloth bears, wild dogs, deer, and monkeys. The tours may include jeep safaris, bird watching, and nature walks to explore the biodiversity of these protected areas. Additionally, visitors can learn about the conservation efforts to protect these species and their habitats. Overall, a wildlife sanctuary tour in Maharashtra can be a fantastic experience for nature enthusiasts and wildlife lovers."

01. Bhima Shankar Wildlife Sanctuary

Bhimashankar Wildlife Sanctuary is a protected area located in the Western Ghats of Maharashtra, India. It covers an area of 120.84 square kilometers and is named after the Bhimashankar Temple, which is located within the sanctuary. The sanctuary is home to several endangered and endemic species... of plants and animals such as the Indian giant squirrel, Malabar grey hornbill, and the Indian rock python.
Visitors can enjoy trekking through the lush forest and spotting various wildlife species, as well as visit the Bhimashankar Temple, a popular pilgrimage site for Hindus. The sanctuary offers a unique experience for nature enthusiasts, wildlife lovers, and pilgrims alike.

02. Karnala Bird Sanctuary

Karnala Bird Sanctuary is a protected area located in the Raigad district of Maharashtra, India. It covers an area of 12.11 square kilometers and is known for its diverse birdlife. The sanctuary is home to over 222 species of birds, including the endangered Ashy minivet, Malabar trogon, and the three-toed kingfisher....Overall, Karnala Bird Sanctuary is a popular destination for nature lovers and birdwatchers. It offers a unique opportunity to observe and appreciate the beauty of nature and its diverse wildlife.

03. Melghat Wildlife Sanctuary

Melghat Wildlife Sanctuary is a very popular reserve well located in Amravati district right on the Satpura Hill Range. The core area and the buffer area of the reserve are formed by the beautiful blend of Gugamal National Park and Chikhaldara Wildlife Sanctuary and it is decently recognised... for the magnificent Tiger Reserve where other wildlife species such as Ussuri Dhole, Indian Leopard and Sloth Bear can be seen living on their natural habitat.

04. Nagzira Wildlife Sanctuary

Nagzira Wildlife Sanctuary is a protected area located in the Bhandara district of Maharashtra, India. It covers an area of 152.81 square kilometers and is home to several endangered species such as the Indian gaur, Bengal tiger, and the Indian leopard. Visitors can enjoy trekking, birdwatching..., and witnessing the beauty of nature and its diverse wildlife in their natural habitat. The sanctuary is covered in dense forests that are home to several species of flora, including teak, bamboo, and medicinal plants.

05. Koyna Wildlife Sanctuary

Koyna Wildlife Sanctuary is a protected area located in the Satara district of Maharashtra, India. It covers an area of 426.39 square kilometers and is known for its diverse flora and fauna. The sanctuary is home to several species of animals such as the Indian bison, Indian giant squirrel, and the Indian leopard....

Koyna Wildlife Sanctuary is also a popular destination for birdwatchers, with over 200 species of birds recorded. The sanctuary is covered in dense forests that are home to several species of flora, including the rare Karvy plant.

06. Sagareshwar Wildlife Sanctuary

Sagareshwar Wildlife Sanctuary is a very renowned and recognised tourist destination located in Sangli District of Maharashtra. It is a great place to visit with your near and dear ones to witness a wide variety of wildlife animals such as deer, wild boar, striped hyena and porcupines living in their natural... habitat.

This sanctuary truly has got a lot of potential to offer to all of its visitors for sure which shall leave you amazed and with awe owing to its certain charm and mesmerising beauty in diversity.

07. Phansad Bird Sanctuary

Phansad Wildlife Sanctuary is a protected area located in the Raigad district of Maharashtra, India. It covers an area of 69.12 square kilometers and is known for its diverse flora and fauna. The sanctuary is home to several species of animals such as the Indian leopard, rusty-spotted... cat, and Indian giant squirrel.

08. Sanjay Gandhi National Park

The park is known for its rich biodiversity, including over 1,300 species of plants and a diverse range of wildlife such as leopards, macaques, deer, and several species of birds. The park also contains several historical sites such as the Kanheri Caves, which are rock-cut...caves and date back to the 1st century BC.

Visitors can enjoy various activities in the park, such as trekking, cycling, and wildlife safaris. The park also has several trails for visitors to explore, with varying degrees of difficulty. There are also several picnic spots and viewpoints where visitors can relax and enjoy the scenic beauty of the park.

09. Chandoli National Park

The park is known for its rich biodiversity, with over 500 species of plants and a diverse range of wildlife such as the Indian giant squirrel, Bengal tiger, Indian leopard, and sloth bear. It is also home to several species of birds such as the grey hornbill, Malabar pied hornbill, and crested serpent eagle....

Overall, Chandoli National Park is a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts, providing an opportunity to witness the beauty of nature and its diverse wildlife in their natural habitat.

10. Malvan Marine Sanctuary

Malvan Marine Sanctuary is a protected area located in the Sindhudurg district of Maharashtra, India. It covers an area of about 29 square kilometers and is known for its diverse marine ecosystem.The sanctuary also contains several coral reefs and rocky outcrops that serve as important... habitats for marine organisms.
It is also a popular destination for birdwatchers, with several species of birds found in the mangrove forests along the coast.
