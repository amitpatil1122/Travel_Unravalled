The Ultimate Foodie Experience


"Food tours in Maharashtra offer a unique and immersive way to explore the diverse culinary landscape of the region. Led by expert guides, these tours take visitors on a journey through local markets, street food stalls, and restaurants, where they can taste and learn about the various traditional and contemporary dishes. From spicy Vada Pav to flavorful Misal Pav, from succulent Kebabs to aromatic Biryanis, food tours in Maharashtra offer a chance to sample some of the most popular and delicious dishes in the state. Along the way, visitors can also learn about the history, culture, and traditions that have shaped Maharashtra's cuisine over the years."

01. Khaugalli - Churchgate Mumbai

Churchgate Khau Galli is a popular street food destination located in the Churchgate area of Mumbai, Maharashtra. It is known for its vibrant and bustling atmosphere, with dozens of food stalls and vendors selling a wide variety of delicious snacks and dishes. Visitors can sample... everything from classic Mumbai street food favorites like Vada Pav, Pav Bhaji, and Bhel Puri, to more unique and modern dishes like cheesy sandwiches, Chinese-style noodles, and loaded fries.

Churchgate Khau Galli is a must-visit destination for foodies and anyone looking to experience the vibrant and diverse food culture of Mumbai.

02. White Biryani - Mumbai

Noor Mohmaddi in Bhendi Bazaar, Mumbai is known for its unique and flavorful White Biryani. Unlike the traditional biryani that is cooked with spices and colored with saffron, the White Biryani is made with a blend of fragrant basmati rice, tender pieces of chicken or mutton,... and a creamy and mildly spiced white sauce.
This dish is said to have originated in the kitchens of the Nizams of Hyderabad and has since become a beloved favorite in Mumbai. The White Biryani at Noor Mohmaddi is a must-try for anyone looking for a delicious and unique biryani experience in the city.

03. Chicken Jumbo Burger - Pune

Earlier the place was called Burger King. No, not the fast-food joint serving American delicacies. It is a small local shop, just as famous and loved as the international giant chain, if not more! The ambience is green and casual but the food is legendary. You will probably have to wait... in a long queue before even getting a place to sit.

Yes, the place is much crowded! The simple, casual, easy on pockets place is on the favourites list of all the Pune folks. for the magnificent Tiger Reserve where other wildlife species such as Ussuri Dhole, Indian Leopard and Sloth Bear can be seen living on their natural habitat.

04. Bedekar Misal - Pune

Misal Pav is a spicy and flavorful dish that is a popular breakfast and snack option in Pune, Maharashtra. It is made with a spiced curry consisting of sprouted lentils or moth beans, topped with crunchy farsan (mixtures of savory snacks), chopped onions, and served with soft pav bread....
The dish is typically garnished with fresh coriander leaves and a squeeze of lime, adding a burst of fresh and tangy flavors. Misal Pav is a staple of Pune's cuisine and is served at various eateries and restaurants across the city. It is a must-try dish for anyone looking to experience the authentic taste of Maharashtra.

05. Zunka Bhakar - Kolhapur

Zunka Bhakar is a popular and traditional dish from the Kolhapur region of Maharashtra. It is a simple yet flavorful dish made with a spicy gram flour (besan) curry, which is typically served with a type of flatbread called Bhakri. The curry is made by cooking besan with onions, garlic, chili..., and a variety of spices, resulting in a thick and savory sauce. The Bhakri is made with jowar or bajra flour, which is rolled into a flatbread and cooked on a griddle. Zunka Bhakar is a hearty and filling dish, perfect for a quick and satisfying meal. It is a staple food of the Kolhapuri cuisine and is widely available in local restaurants and eateries in the region.

06. Kolhapuri Misal Pav

Kolhapur is a food paradise for spicy food lovers. Kolhapuri Misal is one of the famous dishes of Kolhapur. Traditionally a mixture of boiled matki beans, potatoes, farsan, sev and kat (curry), a spicy soup like gravy made with garam masala, onions, tomatoes, garlic, ginger... and coconut. It is usually served with bread or pav. Hot and Spicy are the words which describe this snack to certain extent.

07. Pithla Bhakari - Nashik

Pithla Bhakri is a popular and traditional dish from Nashik, Maharashtra. It is a simple yet comforting dish made with a gram flour (besan) curry, which is typically served with a type of flatbread called Bhakri. The curry is made by cooking besan with onions, garlic, chili, and a variety of spices..., resulting in a thick and savory sauce. The Bhakri is made with jowar or bajra flour, which is rolled into a flatbread and cooked on a griddle. Pithla Bhakri is a staple food of Nashik and is widely available in local restaurants and eateries in the region. It is a hearty and filling dish, perfect for a quick and satisfying meal.

08. Naan Qalia

Naan Qalia is a traditional and popular dish from Aurangabad, Maharashtra. It is a spicy and flavorful mutton curry, which is traditionally cooked in a clay pot over a wood fire. The dish gets its name from the word "qali," which refers to the cooking pot that is used to prepare it....The meat used in Naan Qalia is typically bone-in mutton, which is cooked with a blend of spices and herbs, including ginger, garlic, coriander, cumin, and chili powder. The dish also contains yogurt, which helps to tenderize the meat and add a creamy texture to the curry.

09. Tarri Poha - Nagpur

Tarri Poha is a popular breakfast dish from Nagpur, Maharashtra. It is made with flattened rice (poha), onions, chili, and a spicy gravy called Tarri, which is made with spices and herbs such as cumin, mustard seeds, curry leaves, and red chili powder. The dish is known for its spicy and tangy... flavor, and it is typically served hot.
Tarri Poha is a popular breakfast dish in Nagpur, but it can also be enjoyed as a snack or a light meal. It is a flavorful and satisfying dish that is perfect for those who enjoy spicy and tangy flavors.

10. Santra Barfi

Santra Barfi is a sweet and unique dessert from Nagpur, Maharashtra. It is made with fresh orange pulp, sugar, and milk powder, and is known for its delicious sweet and tangy flavor.Santra Barfi is a popular dessert in Nagpur, and is often served at special occasions and festivals....
Once the Santra Barfi has set, it is cut into small squares or diamond shapes and served. The barfi is soft and creamy with a slightly grainy texture from the milk powder, and has a refreshing and citrusy flavor from the orange pulp.
